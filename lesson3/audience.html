Audience

Target Audience

The main audience of the site is people between 18 and 45 years old, single or married, students and graduates, employees or entrepreneurs. Their interests are: Weather, sports, hiking, camping, leisure, traveling, lakes, beach, swimming, waterfalls, paragliding, skydiving, etc. The main device for accessing the site is the smartphone, however the audience will also access it via computer or tablet.

Personas

Persona 1: The first persona is called Carol. She is 38 years old, married, mother of 3, and a geography teacher. Her interests are: Extreme sports, going to the beach, traveling on weekends and spending time outdoors with her family. She usually accesses the internet through her computer or tablet.

Persona 2: The second persona is named Mike. He is 20 years old, single, plays college football and studies International Relations. His interests are: Motocross, sport shooting, hunting, camping, waterfalls and travel. He usually accesses the internet through his smartphone.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: The client is going to have a day in a lake with all the family and wants to know if It’s going to rain in that day. He or she will navigate to HowIsTheWeather.com and get information about the weather.

Scenario 2: A group of students are going to hike in the mountains and they need to know the weather because they don’t want to go if it’s raining. They can access HowIsTheWeather.com and get information about the weather.

Scenario 3: A mom wants to know if it’s going to rain because she didn’t send her kids to school with an umbrella. She can access HowIsTheWeather.com and get information about the weather.

Scenario 4: A couple heard about a storm coming but they don’t know if it’s true. They will access HowIsTheWeather.com so they can verify the information the storm.

Scenario 5: A parachutist is going to parachute. He would like to know how the wind speed will be and if there is a possibility of rain. He'll pick up his cell phone and go to HowIsTheWeather.com and get this information.
